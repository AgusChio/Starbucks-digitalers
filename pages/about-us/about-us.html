The origin of everything

With the humidity of a cobblestone street; under a luminous sign in the historic Pike Place Market in the city of Seattle, Washington State; with the warmth of a small store and the incomparable perfume of freshly roasted coffee beans; and with a name inspired by a literary classic such as the novel Moby Dick, evoking the maritime tradition of the first merchants; Starbucks opened its doors for the first time as the days went by in March 1971.

Starbucks came to life from that founding essence. Ten years later, a young man from New York, a certain Howard Schultz, dared to enter that store, take a cup of Sumatra and let himself be invaded by its intensity and unpredictable flavor. That instant changed his destiny. A year later he would join the company and in 1983 a tour of Italian coffee shops inspired him to take on a greater challenge: to bring the warmth and art of Italian coffee culture to the heart of the United States.

That challenge took on its own essence. In 1987 the color green displaced the brown of the aprons to build reference forever. Soon Starbucks expanded to Chicago, Vancouver (Canada), California, Washington and New York. In 1996 it crossed the Pacific and landed first in Japan, then in Europe two years later and in China in 1999. The connection was global.

Our history in argentina

If there is a time when any porteño enjoys having a good cup of coffee in the streets of Buenos Aires or in a picturesque neighborhood, that is undoubtedly winter. In the southernmost country in the world, getting together for a coffee is a deep-rooted tradition and is part of the daily social fabric.

That is why, in the cold air of May 30, 2008, when Starbucks opened its first store in Argentina, in Alto Palermo Shopping, the challenge was - and still is - a catching one: to conquer from the experience an exquisite community accustomed to enjoy coffee from every possible perspective.

Thirteen years after that May, that adventure continues and 131 Starbucks stores open their doors every morning throughout the country. With presence in Buenos Aires, Rosario, Córdoba, Neuquén and Mendoza, currently more than 3,500 partners work so that every Argentinean can enjoy the personalization and variety of the Starbucks experience when they visit our stores. And that is, after all, our greatest satisfaction.
